Affordability

Cost a Day

For Myrtle Beach in 2026, a common baseline is $110 for lodging and $74 for meals and incidentals, which comes out to about $184 per day per person. In the busier months, lodging can increase, so the daily baseline can jump closer to $206 to $256 depending on the month. Unfortunately, the best months to go as far as comfortability and things to do will be the more expensive months. But, hey you're going on vacation, so it's to be expected.

That is not a perfect "real life" budget, but it is a nice standard. I usually spend a lot less realistically. Your actual daily cost depends on whether you split lodging with others, eat mostly casual meals, pay for attractions, and how close you stay to the beach. I plan a lot beforehand and I'm not about spending money recklessly, so I may spend around $50-$100 a day. The above estimates are really numbers and stats from online.

One Week Cost

Using the same baseline idea, a one week estimate (7 days) can be around $1,288 per person for months that use the $110 lodging baseline. If you visit in peak summer months where lodging is listed higher, that same one week baseline can move closer to $1,792 per person. Attractions, souvenirs, and premium oceanfront hotels can push it higher. But, remember I believe from my experience that isn't how much it would cost in reality. I thnk it'll cost this much if you don't look for deals and always try to go for the best and most luxurious stuff.

Most Expensive Things

The biggest cost is usually lodging, especially in summer and during big event weekends. Other expensive items are attraction tickets (aquarium, SkyWheel, amusement park, WonderWorks), eating out for every meal, parking close to the busiest areas, and last-minute bookings.

Least Expensive Things

Myrtle Beach can be affordable if you focus on free or low-cost activities. Beach days, walking the boardwalk, watching sunsets, and using public areas are usually the cheapest ways to enjoy the city. You can also save money by staying a bit farther from the beach, cooking some meals, and choosing a few paid attractions instead of doing something ticketed every day.

Potential for living here?

Myrtle Beach can be a good place to live if you like a coastal lifestyle and do well in a tourism-driven area. The tradeoff is that the city is very seasonal, which impacts traffic, crowds, and sometimes local job patterns. Cost of living depends heavily on where you live (closer to the ocean usually costs more) and housing availability.
