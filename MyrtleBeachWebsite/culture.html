Culture (The People)

Overall Culture

Myrtle Beach is very tourist-focused today. A lot of the city revolves around visitors, so the atmosphere is geared toward vacations, convenience, and entertainment. Earlier Myrtle Beach felt more like a small beach community that people traveled to for a simple coastal getaway. You still get that coastal-Southern friendliness in a lot of places, especially once you move away from the busiest strips.

Racial Demographics

Myrtle Beach is diverse, with multiple racial and ethnic groups represented in the local population. The U.S. Census Bureau QuickFacts table for Myrtle Beach city shows a breakdown of: Black(15.0%), White(65.6%), Hispanic/Latino (13.2%), and Asian (1.2%).

Food

Food in Myrtle Beach is a mix of seafood and vacation-friendly options. Seafood is a big deal here, especially shrimp, crab, and fried baskets, but there are also tons of casual choices like burgers, pizza, and boardwalk-style snacks and desserts. I'm not much of a seafood person, so I go for all of the other options myself.

Culture Snapshot

The vibe can change depending on the season. Summer feels busy and energetic, while the shoulder seasons are calmer and more relaxed. People tend to plan around beach weather, events, and whatever is happening on the boardwalk or at Broadway at the Beach. I love outside activities and being warm, so coming during the summer months is what I prefer.

Local People

Locals are usually laid-back and practical. Many people work in hospitality, food service, retail, or industries that support tourism. Outside of peak season, it feels more like a normal coastal community with routines and regular neighborhoods.

Tourists

Most tourists are families, couples, and groups just trying to have a good vacation. Like any major tourist city, behavior depends on the time of year. Holiday weekends and peak summer nights can get louder and more crowded, while spring and fall usually feel easier and less hectic. If you ever traveled and been around a lot of tourists, or a lot of tourists come to your city for whatever reason, I think you can get a good idea of how things may be. I, personally, haven't seen or heard of many issues at Myrtle Beach.

Events

Myrtle Beach hosts major events that bring in visitors and shape the culture of the area. One example is the Carolina Country Music Fest (CCMF), which is a large music festival held on the oceanfront and boardwalk. Events like this help define the city as a seasonal destination with big crowd weekends.
