Weather and Environment

What's the weather like?

Myrtle Beach does experience all four seasons, but they are usually mild compared to many other parts of the country. Winters are cool and not extreme most of the time, spring warms up quickly, summers are hot and humid, and fall stays warm longer than inland areas. You can expect the hottest and most humid weather in summer, and the coolest temperatures in winter.

Best Months to Visit

The best months to visit are usually spring and early fall. Those months are warm enough to still feel like a beach trip, but not as hot and crowded as peak summer. It can also be easier to find better prices and availability outside of June through August. If you visit during hurricane season (June 1 to November 30), it is smart to watch forecasts and have flexible plans.

Geography

Myrtle Beach is mostly low and flat coastal land. The environment is ocean-focused, with long sandy beaches, dunes, and coastline communities along the Grand Strand. You are not dealing with mountains or steep hills here. The area is built around the Atlantic Ocean, so the beach and shoreline are the main natural features that shape the city.

Quick Tips

  • Summer: Plan indoor attractions mid-day (heat or rain), and beach time in the morning or evening.
  • Spring/Fall: Best balance of comfort and crowds.
  • Winter: Quieter and cooler, but still walkable for boardwalk and beach views.
